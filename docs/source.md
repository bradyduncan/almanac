## Micro-learning loop

5 days/week, 10–15 min/session. Rotate through priorities (3 → 4 → 8 → 6 → 9), then sprinkle
10, 1, 7, 5, and brief 2. Each session: 3–5 min read/recall principles, 5–10 min one drill.

## 3. Social calibration & nonverbal game (top priority)

**Core idea:** You're training "how you come across" on default settings.

**What to know**
- First impressions form within a few seconds and are heavily influenced by nonverbal cues (posture, eye contact, facial expression, grooming, clothing).
- Nonverbal channels (body language, tone) often carry more weight than the literal words, especially for judgments of warmth and competence.
- People read a combination of face and body together when inferring traits like dominance, trustworthiness, and approachability.

**Micro-drills (5–10 min)**
- **Mirror check** — `rehearsal`, 2 min. Practice a relaxed, upright posture, neutral-friendly face, steady eye contact for 2 minutes; aim for "approachable but confident."
- **First-10-seconds drill** — `record_review`, 10 min. Record yourself walking into frame, greeting an imaginary group, and sitting down; review for fidgeting, slouch, vocal energy.
- **Social scanning** — `audit`, 5 min. On campus or in a café, silently label body language of strangers (open/closed, engaged/bored) to sharpen pattern recognition.

## 4. Assertiveness, boundaries, self-advocacy

**Core idea:** Communicate what you want clearly, without being passive or aggressive.

**What to know**
- Assertiveness means expressing your views and needs directly and respectfully; it reduces stress and resentment.
- Key tools: "I" statements, specific requests, and the ability to say no without over-explaining.
- Your style may skew passive or aggressive in different contexts (friends vs professors vs coworkers); recognizing this is step one.

**Micro-drills**
- **Style audit** — `audit`, 5 min. Write 3 recent situations where you felt walked on or frustrated; label how you responded (passive/aggressive/assertive).
- **Script 3 sentences** — `script`, 5 min. One "I feel… when… I'd like…" statement, one clear no ("No, I can't do that now"), and one boundary ("I'm not available after 8 pm for work messages").
- **Role-play in your head** — `rehearsal`, 5 min. Take a common stress scenario (group-project scope creep, weekend plan you don't want) and rehearse an assertive 2–3 sentence response.

## 8. Conflict resolution & negotiation (life + work)

**Core idea:** Handle disagreements without drama and get better outcomes.

**What to know**
- Effective conflict resolution starts with early detection, understanding root causes, and choosing a constructive process rather than avoiding or escalating.
- Foundational negotiation ideas: separate people from the problem, look for shared interests, avoid purely positional standoffs.
- Managing emotions (yours and theirs) is central — staying calm, pausing when provoked, not getting dragged into power plays.

**Micro-drills**
- **5-step reflection** — `reflection`, 5 min. After any minor conflict, jot down what triggered it, what each side wanted, what you did, and what you'd try next time.
- **Reframe practice** — `reflection`, 5 min. Take a blaming thought ("He's lazy") and reframe it as an interest or problem ("He's overloaded; we need a clearer task split").
- **Negotiation pattern** — `script`, 5 min. Write a mini-script where you (1) state the issue, (2) state your ideal, (3) propose a trade ("If we ship v1 by Friday, I'll own the demo deck").

## 6. Hosting, cooking, and hospitality

**Core idea:** Be the guy who can host people comfortably, not just "have people over."

**What to know**
- Practical life-skills lists emphasize basic cooking, home skills, and being able to host as markers of adult competence.
- Knowing one or two "signature" dishes and a simple drink shows intention and care, which matters socially and romantically.
- Hosting etiquette includes greeting people, making introductions, and managing environment (music, lighting, cleanliness).

**Micro-drills**
- **Recipe standardization** — `checklist`, 10 min. Pick one dish (e.g., seafood pasta) and write an ultra-short version: ingredients, 5–7 steps, timing; refine after each cook.
- **Host checklist** — `checklist`, 5 min. Make a 10-item list to run before people arrive: surfaces cleared, bathroom check, trash, simple snacks, playlist, etc.
- **Intros practice** — `script`, 5 min. Script 3–4 variations of introducing two people ("Hey X, this is Y — we worked together on a cloud project; Y's big into golf too").

## 9. Long-view, failing well, and choosing bets

**Core idea:** Train your time horizon and decision filters.

**What to know**
- A long-view lens helps you invest your 20s in skills, relationships, and health that compound instead of chasing short-term validation.
- Learning to say yes/no strategically — based on your values and long-term direction — is a core skill in this decade.
- "Failing well" means debriefing, adjusting systems, and not letting one outcome define your identity.

**Micro-drills**
- **Year filters** — `reflection`, 5 min. Write 3 questions you'll use before new commitments (e.g., "Does this align with my 3-year direction?", "Will I learn something valuable?").
- **Failure post-mortem** — `reflection`, 5 min. Pick one recent "L" and write a 5-bullet debrief: what happened, root cause, what to keep, what to change, what to watch for next time.
- **Compounding scan** — `audit`, 5 min. List 5 current habits; mark each +1 (compounds), 0 (neutral), or −1 (erodes) over 5+ years.

## 10. Etiquette & formal-setting presence

**Core idea:** Perform under "formal" expectations without awkwardness.

**What to know**
- Modern men's life-skills lists emphasize understanding dress codes, table manners, and general event etiquette (phone away, punctuality).
- Basic conversation skills — small talk, introductions, giving a short toast — are repeatedly called out as key adult competencies.
- Remembering details and following up (thank-you messages, check-ins) leaves a strong impression.

**Micro-drills**
- **Place-setting visualization** — `rehearsal`, 3 min. Mentally rehearse standard Western table layout (forks left, knives/spoons right, outer to inner).
- **30-second toast** — `script`, 5 min. Draft one short, sincere toast for a friend's birthday or achievement; time it under 45 seconds.
- **Follow-up templates** — `script`, 5 min. Write 2 quick messages — one post-interview thank-you, one "great meeting you at X, let's grab coffee sometime."

## 1. Male health edge in your 20s

**Core idea:** You're front-loading protection for your 30s–40s.

**What to know**
- Guidelines highlight keeping BMI under ~25, limiting trans/saturated fats, and exercising most days as key male health levers.
- Annual physicals, BP checks, and STI screening (if sexually active) are recommended even when you feel fine.
- Early attention to mental health (sleep, mood, motivation shifts) prevents entrenched issues later.

**Micro-drills**
- **Metric baseline** — `audit`, 10 min. Log current stats (weight, resting HR if available, weekly exercise, average sleep) and one small tweak goal.
- **Question bank** — `checklist`, 10 min. Draft 5 questions for your next physical (e.g., family risk, lab frequency, sleep concerns).

## 7. Basic home, car, and travel competence

**Core idea:** General self-reliance.

**What to know**
- Skills lists for men and young adults repeatedly include simple home maintenance (tools, breaker, drains, sewing a button) and basic car skills.
- Competence with travel logistics (reading signage, public transit, checking safety info) is core to independent travel.

**Micro-drills**
- **Demo + recall** — `reflection`, 5 min. Watch one 5-minute demo on changing a tire or resetting a breaker, then write the steps from memory.
- **Tool familiarity** — `checklist`, 5 min. Make a minimal toolkit list (screwdrivers, hammer, pliers, wrench, tape) and note at least one use for each.
- **Transit literacy** — `audit`, 5 min. Pick a city you like; read its subway/tram map plus how to buy a ticket.

## 5. Style, grooming, and presenting well

**Core idea:** Clean, intentional, context-appropriate.

**What to know**
- Modern "skills men need" lists emphasize understanding how to buy a suit, dress for occasions, and maintain clothes (washing, ironing).
- Grooming (hair, facial hair, skincare) and fit matter more than brand for perceived attractiveness and professionalism.

**Micro-drills**
- **Fit audit** — `record_review`, 5 min. Put on your most formal outfit, take front/side photos; check shoulder fit, sleeve length, pant break.
- **Grooming checklist** — `checklist`, 5 min. Build a weekly routine: hair, beard/neckline, nails, brows, simple skincare steps.
- **Occasion mapping** — `reflection`, 5 min. List 5 scenarios (date, interview, tech conference, golf, family dinner); note a specific outfit for each.

## 2. First aid & emergencies (intentionally light)

**Core idea:** Minimal time, maximum upside.

**What to know**
- General life-skills resources emphasize basic first aid, choking response, and emergency awareness as essential adult skills.

**Micro-drills (very short)**
- **Protocol review** — `reflection`, 5 min. Once per week, review one protocol — choking, CPR basics, or bleeding control (a reputable infographic or short video) — and mentally rehearse.
- **Exit walk** — `audit`, 5 min. Walk your building and locate exits, fire extinguishers, and AEDs so you aren't guessing under stress.
