## 1. Personal finance foundations
- 50/30/20 framework: ~50% needs, 30% wants, 20% saving/debt payoff as a simple budget baseline.
- Build an emergency fund of 3–6 months of living expenses over time; a practical first goal is $500–1000.
- Start investing early (even small amounts) into broad, low-fee index funds or retirement accounts; compound growth matters more than picking the "perfect" investment.

## 2. Health and preventive care in your 20s
- Prioritize 7–9 hours of sleep, regular exercise, mostly whole foods — the biggest long-term health levers in your 20s.
- Establish an annual physical and routine STI screening if sexually active, even when you feel fine.
- Limit smoking and alcohol; both tie to later cardiovascular and cancer risk, and cutting back now compounds over decades.

## 3. Nutrition and macros
- Macronutrients: carbs are primary energy, protein supports muscle repair and enzymes, fats support hormones, cellular health, and vitamin absorption.
- Focus on protein and mostly unsaturated fats (nuts, seeds, olive oil, fatty fish); minimize trans and excess saturated fats.
- Micronutrients matter for immunity, bones, energy; a varied diet (fruits, vegetables, whole grains, some dairy or fortified alternatives) covers most needs.

## 4. Strength training basics
- Beginner approach: full-body 2–4×/week hitting major movement patterns (squat, hinge, push, pull, carry).
- Progressive overload — gradually increasing weight, reps, or difficulty — is the key driver, not constantly changing exercises.
- Technique and recovery (sleep, calories, protein) matter as much as the program for avoiding injury and sustainable progress.

## 5. Sleep hygiene
- Consistent sleep/wake time daily, including weekends; circadian rhythm thrives on regularity.
- Wind-down routine: avoid bright screens, heavy meals, intense problem-solving 30–60 min before bed.
- Optimize environment: dark, cool, quiet room with minimal light leakage and noise.

## 6. Digital privacy and security
- Strong, unique passwords per important account, stored in a password manager; cuts credential-stuffing risk.
- Enable MFA (prefer app-based or hardware keys) on email, banking, major social accounts.
- Be intentional about shared data: review app permissions and privacy settings; location, financial activity, and browsing habits all have value.

## 7. Emotional intelligence and self-awareness
- EI = perceiving, understanding, regulating, and responding to emotions in self and others; underpins collaboration and leadership.
- Components: self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship management — all trainable with reflection and feedback.
- Active listening, naming your own emotions, and stress-management techniques measurably improve EI over time.

## 8. Communication in relationships
- Choose appropriate times to discuss conflict; be honest but respectful; don't hide feelings until they boil over.
- Validate the other's perspective, stay on a single topic instead of stacking grievances, accept constructive criticism — reduces drama.
- Calmly negotiating compromise beats "winning" arguments, romantic and professional.

## 9. Early-career thinking (20s)
- Career trajectories are rarely linear; build skills, networks, and experiences over chasing titles.
- Treat each role/internship/project as a way to learn and test hypotheses about what energizes you.
- Balance ambition with enjoying the process to prevent burnout and stay open to better opportunities.

## 10. Civic basics (voting & adulthood logistics)
- Know how to register and vote where you live; local and national decisions affect housing, education, tech policy.
- Understand at a high level how taxes, benefits, and basic legal responsibilities work in your country/state in your 20s.
- Stay informed on major local/national issues from a few reliable sources, not just social feeds.
