title: Social calibration & nonverbal game
default_priority: 1
core_idea: You're training how you come across on default settings.
lessons:
  - title: First impressions form in seconds
    body: |
      People form a first impression of you within a few seconds of meeting — often
      before you've said anything meaningful. That snap judgment is driven mostly by
      nonverbal cues: posture, eye contact, facial expression, grooming, and how you're
      dressed. It's sticky, too — later evidence gets filtered through that first read,
      so the cheapest place to improve how you come across is the opening moment.

      Practical upshot: walk in with relaxed, upright posture, an open expression, and
      unhurried movement. You're not performing confidence; you're removing the signals
      (slouch, fidget, darting eyes) that read as anxious or low-status.
  - title: Warmth and competence
    body: |
      Most social judgments collapse onto two axes: warmth (are you friendly, safe,
      well-intentioned?) and competence (are you capable, sharp, effective?). People
      read these from your face and body together — a warm face with closed, tense body
      language sends a mixed signal.

      Aim to project both: warmth through eye contact, a genuine smile, and open
      posture; competence through stillness, steady pace, and taking up appropriate
      space. Most people lean too hard one way — train the side you neglect.
activities:
  - title: Mirror check
    kind: rehearsal
    est_minutes: 2
    instructions: Relaxed upright posture, neutral-friendly face, steady eye contact for two minutes. Aim for "approachable but confident."
  - title: First-10-seconds drill
    kind: record_review
    est_minutes: 10
    instructions: Record yourself walking into frame, greeting an imaginary group, and sitting down. Review for fidgeting, slouch, and vocal energy.
  - title: Social scanning
    kind: audit
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: In a café or on campus, silently label the body language of strangers (open/closed, engaged/bored) for five minutes to sharpen pattern recognition.
  - title: Quiz — what drives first impressions
    kind: quiz
    est_minutes: 1
    prompt: First impressions are driven mostly by which channel?
    choices:
      - The literal content of what you say
      - Nonverbal cues like posture, expression, and eye contact
      - Your job title or credentials
      - How long you talk
    answer_index: 1
  - title: Quiz — the two judgment axes
    kind: quiz
    est_minutes: 1
    prompt: Most social judgments collapse onto which two axes?
    choices:
      - Wealth and humor
      - Intelligence and attractiveness
      - Warmth and competence
      - Dominance and age
    answer_index: 2
