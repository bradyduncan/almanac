---
slug: social-calibration
title: Social calibration & nonverbal game
default_priority: 1
core_idea: You're training how you come across on default settings.
facts:
  - First impressions form within a few seconds and are heavily influenced by nonverbal cues — posture, eye contact, facial expression, grooming, clothing.
  - Nonverbal channels (body language, tone) often carry more weight than the literal words, especially for judgments of warmth and competence.
  - People read face and body together when inferring traits like dominance, trustworthiness, and approachability.
drills:
  - title: Mirror check
    kind: rehearsal
    est_minutes: 2
    instructions: Practice a relaxed, upright posture, neutral-friendly face, and steady eye contact for two minutes. Aim for "approachable but confident."
  - title: First-10-seconds drill
    kind: record_review
    est_minutes: 10
    instructions: Record yourself walking into frame, greeting an imaginary group, and sitting down. Review for fidgeting, slouch, and vocal energy.
  - title: Social scanning
    kind: audit
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: In a café or on campus, silently label the body language of strangers (open/closed, engaged/bored) for five minutes to sharpen pattern recognition.
---

Top-priority domain. The goal is to make your default, unconscious presentation read as warm
and competent — before you say anything.
