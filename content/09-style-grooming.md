title: Style, grooming & presenting well
default_priority: 9
core_idea: Clean, intentional, context-appropriate.
levels:
  beginner:
    lessons:
      - title: Fit beats brand
        body: |
          The single biggest lever in how clothes look on you is fit, not label or price. A
          modest outfit that fits at the shoulders, sleeves, and waist reads better than an
          expensive one that doesn't. When you check an outfit, look specifically at shoulder
          seams (should sit at your shoulder, not droop), sleeve and pant length, and the
          pant "break" at the shoe.

          Learn the basics of maintaining clothes too — washing correctly, ironing, knowing
          when something needs a tailor. Caring for what you own beats constantly buying new.
      - title: Grooming and dressing for the occasion
        body: |
          Grooming — hair, facial hair and neckline, nails, brows, simple skincare — moves
          perceived attractiveness and professionalism more than most people expect, and a
          light weekly routine keeps it consistent.

          The other half is context: the right outfit depends on the occasion. Map your
          common scenarios (date, interview, conference, casual, family dinner) to a specific
          go-to outfit so you're never guessing the morning of.
    activities:
      - title: Fit audit
        kind: record_review
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: Put on your most formal outfit and take front and side photos; check shoulder fit, sleeve length, and pant break.
      - title: Grooming checklist
        kind: checklist
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: Build a weekly routine covering hair, beard/neckline, nails, brows, and simple skincare steps.
      - title: Occasion mapping
        kind: reflection
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: List 5 scenarios (date, interview, tech conference, golf, family dinner) and note a specific outfit for each.
      - title: Quiz — the biggest lever
        kind: quiz
        est_minutes: 1
        prompt: What matters most in how clothing looks on you?
        choices:
          - The brand on the label
          - How much it cost
          - How well it fits
          - How new it is
        answer_index: 2
      - title: Quiz — checking fit
        kind: quiz
        est_minutes: 1
        prompt: When auditing fit, which detail should you check first?
        choices:
          - The color
          - Whether the shoulder seams sit at your shoulders
          - The price tag
          - The fabric brand
        answer_index: 1
