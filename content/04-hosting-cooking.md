title: Hosting, cooking & hospitality
default_priority: 4
core_idea: Be the guy who can host people comfortably, not just have people over.
levels:
  beginner:
    lessons:
      - title: Hosting is care made visible
        body: |
          Hosting well isn't about a big budget — it's about visible care. Greeting people
          at the door, taking their coat, making introductions, and managing the
          environment (lighting low and warm, music present but not loud, surfaces clean)
          tells guests you thought about them before they arrived.

          The host sets the emotional weather. If you're relaxed and attentive, the room
          relaxes. Do the prep beforehand so you're not stuck in the kitchen while everyone
          else mingles.
      - title: A signature dish and a simple drink
        body: |
          You don't need a wide repertoire — one or two dishes you can make reliably, plus
          one simple drink, signals intention and competence. Standardize them: write the
          recipe down, time it, and refine after each cook until it's automatic.

          Knowing your dish cold frees your attention for your guests instead of the stove,
          and "I'll cook for you" becomes a move you can actually make.
    activities:
      - title: Recipe standardization
        kind: checklist
        est_minutes: 10
        instructions: Pick one dish (e.g., seafood pasta) and write an ultra-short version — ingredients, 5–7 steps, timing. Refine it after each cook.
      - title: Host checklist
        kind: checklist
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: Make a 10-item list to run before people arrive — surfaces cleared, bathroom check, trash out, simple snacks, playlist, and so on.
      - title: Intros practice
        kind: script
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: 'Script 3–4 variations of introducing two people ("Hey X, this is Y — we worked together on a cloud project; Y''s big into golf too").'
      - title: Quiz — what makes hosting work
        kind: quiz
        est_minutes: 1
        prompt: According to the lesson, good hosting depends most on...
        choices:

          - Visible care — greeting, intros, and managing the environment
          - Having the largest apartment
          - Cooking something complicated
        answer_index: 1
      - title: Quiz — the signature dish
        kind: quiz
        est_minutes: 1
        prompt: Why standardize one or two signature dishes?
        choices:

          - Because variety is required to host
          - To avoid ever cooking the same thing twice
        answer_index: 1
