---
slug: assertiveness
title: Assertiveness, boundaries & self-advocacy
default_priority: 2
core_idea: Communicate what you want clearly, without being passive or aggressive.
facts:
  - Assertiveness means expressing your views and needs directly and respectfully; it reduces stress and resentment.
  - Key tools are "I" statements, specific requests, and the ability to say no without over-explaining.
  - Your style may skew passive or aggressive in different contexts (friends vs professors vs coworkers); recognizing this is step one.
drills:
  - title: Style audit
    kind: audit
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Write 3 recent situations where you felt walked on or frustrated, and label how you responded — passive, aggressive, or assertive.
  - title: Script 3 sentences
    kind: script
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: 'Write one "I feel… when… I''d like…" statement, one clean no ("No, I can''t do that now"), and one boundary ("I''m not available after 8 pm for work messages").'
  - title: Role-play in your head
    kind: rehearsal
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Take a common stress scenario (group-project scope creep, a weekend plan you don't want) and rehearse an assertive 2–3 sentence response.
