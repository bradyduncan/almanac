default_priority: 7
core_idea: You're front-loading protection for your 30s and 40s.
lessons:
  - title: The big levers
    body: |
      A few habits do most of the work for long-term male health: 7–9 hours of sleep,
      moving most days, mostly whole foods, and keeping body composition in a healthy
      range (guidelines often cite BMI under ~25 as a rough marker). Limiting trans and
      excess saturated fats, smoking, and heavy drinking compounds in your favor over
      decades.

      None of this is dramatic. The point is that small, consistent inputs now buy
      outsized protection later — you're front-loading your 30s and 40s.
  - title: Screening and mental health
    body: |
      Feeling fine isn't proof you're fine. An annual physical, blood-pressure checks,
      and STI screening (if sexually active) catch the things that are silent until
      they're not. Walk in with a few questions ready — family risk, what labs make
      sense, anything you've noticed.

      Mental health is part of the same picture. Track sleep, mood, and motivation;
      catching a downward shift early prevents it from becoming entrenched.
