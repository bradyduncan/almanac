slug: long-view
title: Long-view, failing well & choosing bets
default_priority: 5
core_idea: Train your time horizon and decision filters.
lessons:
  - title: Compounding in your 20s
    body: |
      The highest-leverage moves in your 20s are the ones that compound: skills,
      relationships, health, and reputation all grow on themselves over time. A long-view
      lens means weighting those over short-term validation — likes, status hits, easy
      wins that don't build anything.

      Run new commitments through a simple filter: does this align with where I want to
      be in a few years, and will I learn something durable? Saying yes and no on purpose
      — not by default or by fear of missing out — is the core skill of the decade.
  - title: Failing well
    body: |
      You will fail; the question is whether you extract value from it. Failing well
      means debriefing honestly, adjusting the system that produced the failure, and not
      letting one outcome define your identity. A bad result is data, not a verdict.

      The debrief is the mechanism: what happened, what caused it, what to keep, what to
      change, what to watch for next time. Do that and failures become tuition rather
      than scars.
activities:
  - title: Year filters
    kind: reflection
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: 'Write 3 questions you''ll use before new commitments (e.g., "Does this align with my 3-year direction?", "Will I learn something valuable?").'
  - title: Failure post-mortem
    kind: reflection
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: 'Pick one recent "L" and write a 5-bullet debrief: what happened, root cause, what to keep, what to change, what to watch for next time.'
  - title: Compounding scan
    kind: audit
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: List 5 current habits and mark each +1 (compounds), 0 (neutral), or −1 (erodes) over a 5+ year horizon.
  - title: Quiz — what to optimize for
    kind: quiz
    est_minutes: 1
    prompt: A long-view lens means weighting which kind of investment?
    choices:
      - Short-term validation and status hits
      - Things that compound — skills, relationships, health
      - Whatever feels easiest today
      - Whatever others are doing
    answer_index: 1
  - title: Quiz — failing well
    kind: quiz
    est_minutes: 1
    prompt: "\"Failing well\" is best described as..."
    choices:
      - Avoiding any situation where you might fail
      - Debriefing, fixing the system, and not letting it define you
      - Forgetting the failure as fast as possible
      - Blaming external factors
    answer_index: 1
