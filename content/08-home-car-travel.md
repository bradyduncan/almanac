title: Basic home, car & travel competence
default_priority: 8
core_idea: General self-reliance.
lessons:
  - title: Home and car basics
    body: |
      A small set of practical skills covers most of daily self-reliance: using basic
      tools, finding and resetting the breaker, clearing a slow drain, sewing a button,
      and the car essentials — checking tire pressure, changing a tire, jumping a dead
      battery. You don't need to be a mechanic; you need to not be helpless when
      something small goes wrong.

      Build a minimal toolkit (screwdrivers, hammer, pliers, an adjustable wrench, tape)
      and know one use for each. Competence here is mostly having done the thing once,
      calmly, before you need it under pressure.
  - title: Travel logistics
    body: |
      Independent travel is a logistics skill: reading signage, navigating public
      transit, buying the right ticket, and checking basic safety info for where you're
      going. The first time in a new system feels chaotic; a few minutes of prep — how
      the metro works, how to pay — removes most of the friction.

      The transferable habit is orienting fast: find the map, find how to pay, find the
      exit. Do that and most cities become navigable.
activities:
  - title: Demo + recall
    kind: reflection
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Watch one 5-minute demo on changing a tire or resetting a breaker, then write the steps from memory.
  - title: Tool familiarity
    kind: checklist
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Make a minimal toolkit list (screwdrivers, hammer, pliers, wrench, tape) and note at least one use for each item.
  - title: Transit literacy
    kind: audit
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Pick a city you like and spend five minutes reading its subway or tram map plus how to buy a ticket.
