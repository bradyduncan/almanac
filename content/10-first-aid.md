lessons:
  - title: Why a little first aid pays off
    body: |
      This domain is intentionally light: a small time investment with potentially huge
      payoff. You don't need formal certification to be useful in the first minutes of an
      emergency — knowing the basic responses to choking, cardiac arrest, and serious
      bleeding, plus staying calm enough to act, is most of the value.

      Treat it as periodic review, not mastery. Skimming a reputable protocol now and
      then keeps the steps accessible when adrenaline makes clear thinking hard.
  - title: The core protocols, briefly
    body: |
      A rough mental map: for choking, abdominal thrusts (Heimlich) on a conscious adult
      who can't breathe or speak. For suspected cardiac arrest, call emergency services
      and start hands-only CPR — hard, fast chest compressions. For serious bleeding,
      firm direct pressure on the wound.

      These are pointers, not training — follow a certified course or a reputable source
      for the real steps. The goal here is to know that a response exists and roughly
      what it is, so you're not starting from zero.
activities:
  - title: Protocol review
    kind: reflection
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Once per week, review one protocol — choking, CPR basics, or bleeding control — from a reputable infographic or short video, and mentally rehearse it.
  - title: Exit walk
    kind: audit
    est_minutes: 5
    instructions: Walk your building and locate exits, fire extinguishers, and AEDs so you aren't guessing under stress.
