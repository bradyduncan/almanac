title: Etiquette & formal-setting presence
default_priority: 6
core_idea: Perform under formal expectations without awkwardness.
levels:
  beginner:
    lessons:
      - title: The mechanics of formal settings
        body: |
          Formal settings run on a handful of conventions: read the dress code, know basic
          table manners, keep your phone away, and show up on time. None of it is hard — it
          just has to be automatic so you're not visibly figuring it out in the moment.

          Table basics: work outer to inner with utensils, forks on the left, knives and
          spoons on the right. Knowing the layout means one less thing to think about while
          you focus on the people.
      - title: Conversation and follow-up
        body: |
          The social half is small talk, introductions, and the occasional short toast.
          These are repeatable: have a couple of openers, learn to introduce two people with
          a hook ("you both ski"), and be able to give a sincere 30-second toast.

          What separates a strong impression from a forgettable one is follow-up —
          remembering a detail and sending a quick thank-you or check-in afterward. Cheap to
          do, rare in practice, disproportionately memorable.
    activities:
      - title: Place-setting visualization
        kind: rehearsal
        est_minutes: 3
        instructions: Mentally rehearse a standard Western table layout — forks on the left, knives and spoons on the right, working outer to inner.
      - title: 30-second toast
        kind: script
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: Draft one short, sincere toast for a friend's birthday or achievement; time it so it stays under 45 seconds.
      - title: Follow-up templates
        kind: script
        est_minutes: 5
        instructions: 'Write 2 quick messages — one post-interview thank-you and one "great meeting you at X, let''s grab coffee sometime."'
      - title: Quiz — utensil order
        kind: quiz
        est_minutes: 1
        prompt: At a formal place setting, how do you use the utensils?
        choices:
          - Inner to outer
          - Outer to inner, as courses arrive
          - Whatever is closest
          - Forks on the right, knives on the left
        answer_index: 1
      - title: Quiz — leaving an impression
        kind: quiz
        est_minutes: 1
        prompt: What most reliably separates a strong impression from a forgettable one?
        choices:
          - Talking the most at the table
          - Wearing the most expensive outfit
          - Remembering a detail and following up afterward
          - Giving the longest toast
        answer_index: 2
